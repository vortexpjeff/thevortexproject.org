What this is

The Vortex Project is a long-term experiment in living with a place — not just on it. Pine Hollow is a narrow Appalachian hollow with steep wooded slopes, a creek draining south-southwest toward the Smokies, and a billion years of geology underfoot.

We're building a low-cost edge bioacoustics station for continuous biodiversity monitoring, combining BirdNET-Pi, custom insect classifiers, local storage, and human review to create reviewed acoustic datasets from field recordings. The system runs 24/7. The data accumulates on its own schedule. The cicadas sing when they sing.

This is not a smart home. It's a commonwealth: human, animal, machine, land. Every part has its own voice and its own edges. The project is the relationship, not the outcome.
