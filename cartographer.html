Reading the ledger

Activity, not certainty

USGS earthquakes match the selected UTC day. Wildfires use the selected day plus seven prior days because active fire records may not receive a new geometry every day. Other NASA EONET categories use two prior days. Every row retains its provider timestamp.

Prescribed and RX burns are excluded. To keep dense regions legible, the frame maps a bounded recent sample per category while the ledger retains every filtered record. Events south of 66°S remain in the ledger but fall outside the orbital image crop.

Method and boundary

What this surface shows

Source-native observation

Corrected reflectance combines visible channels to approximate daylight human vision. Cloud, snow, bright ground, orbital seams, and missing passes remain part of the evidence. A static NASA Blue Marble baseline appears only when the selected daily image cannot be decoded, and is labeled as timeless rather than current.

Global context, no private marker

This public surface contains no station marker, property boundary, Observatory coordinates, camera frame, or raw archive material. Cartographer reads global public providers; the project archive remains the reviewed evidence backbone for local layers elsewhere in the project.
